The Great Escape (Prison break)

Allied prisoners of war plan for several hundred of their number to escape
from a German camp during World War II.
Based on a true story, a group of allied escape artist-type prisoners-of-war
are all put in an "escape proof" camp. Their leader decides to try to take out
several hundred all at once. The first half of the movie is played for comedy,
as the prisoners mostly outwit their jailers to dig the escape.
The second half is high adventure as they use planes, trains & boats
to get out of occupied Europe.
